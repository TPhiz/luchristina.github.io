Cauldron

A grocery and recipe app aimed to help shoppers save time at the store.

About the project

The problem: Grocery shopping is a task that some might not find exciting. Many times people could spend more time in a grocery store than necessary.

The goal: To create a mobile prototype that helps people shop for groceries efficiently.

Project details

Role: UX/Product Designer
Tasks: UX Design: Research, sketches, wireframes, branding, mockups, prototypes, usability testing
Duration: ~ 1 month
Tools: Figma, Usability Hub, Google Suite
Deliverables: Mobile application

Research

To begin my research, I deployed a survey to gather various experiences through grocery shopping.

I wanted to know what grocery shoppers were not happy about. This allows me to understand what the underlying issues are. From the chart above these frustrations all relate to time spent.

This question was regarding the method of which a shopper saves their grocery lists. 56% of this sample saves it using their phone's note app (ie. iPhone Notes). Surprisingly, the second most-chosen option was 'memory'. Still, 73% of this sample relies on physically or digitally writing down their lists, which means there is a large market for this niche.

Personas

The Average Cook

Frustrations: She currently saves her grocery list on her phone's Notes app. Nothing is categorized and she constantly goes to the same aisle multiple times to get all of her items.

Goals: She would love an app that organizes her grocery list by type, so that she knows what items would be in a certain aisle or part of the store.

The Party Host

Frustrations: Caryn likes to cook for her potluck parties, but her friends bring fast food. She needs to find a compromise with her friends in order to get rid of the contstant fast food that keeps showing up to the party!

Goals: To compromise, Caryn is willing to cook all the food if her friends buy the groceries. She would like an app that has a sharing function to fix her fast-food issue!

Brand Design

The Brand Name

After thinking of many lackluster names... I decided on Cauldron. The mysticality of the name makes it interesting. When you think of the word cauldron this might come to your mind:
  • A melting pot full of different items
  • People creating a concoction/recipe

Font Selection

Because there is a lot of information within the app, I wanted to keep the font simple. The font I chose is IBM Plex Sans. While it is nice and simple there is still character to this sans-serif font.

Logos


These logos above were the contenders. I wanted to try showing a horizontal view as well as an aerial view of the cauldron. In order to make a final decision, I did some preference testing.

High Fidelity Mockups

The first iteration consisted of including all the screens needed, but they needed to be designed better and designed of higher quality.
The second iteration consisted of layout rearrangements. This transformed each screen dramatically.
The third iteration was related to accessibility issues. For example, the search bar used to be a semi-transparent white with a medium grey text. Unfortunately, that was not accessible. I changed the background to a mandarin color with a darker grey font, and that passes AAA accessibility.

I performed usability testing after the high fidelity mockups were complete to see the users' experience of the app. There were two ways a user could create lists/recipes, and I noticed both ways were used. This is great because the quicker a user can learn how to navigate an app, the better the experience it will be for them.

Changes I made included making a link more contrasted against a photo background, adjusting the home page to provide a call to action and to make it look more professional, as well as increasing the opacity in the search bar for better visibility.

Conclusion

There were many iterations in order to create a good minimum viable product. Layouts are very important, and a layout that one thinks would look best at first may not be the most suitable in the end.

What I would've done different

Keeping accessibility in mind from the beginning is very important and can save a lot of time during screen updates.

If I had more time

I would like to deploy this product and really test to see how much time a user saves using this app compare to their usual method!
