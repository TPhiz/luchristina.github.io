Cauldron

A grocery and recipe app aimed to help shoppers save time at the store.

About the project

The problem Grocery shopping is a task that some might not find exciting. Many times people could spend more time in a grocery store than necessary.

The goal To create a mobile prototype that helps people shop for groceries efficiently.

Project details

Role UX/Product Designer
Tasks UX Design: Research, sketches, wireframes, branding, mockups, prototypes, usability testing
Duration ~ 1 month
Tools Figma, Usability Hub, Google Suite
Deliverables Mobile application

Research

To begin my research, I deployed a survey to gather various experiences through grocery shopping.

I wanted to understand the frustrations of grocery shopping. These reasons all relate to time spent..

I asked what shoppers use when they need to take note of their grocery list. 73% of this sample stated that they use a physical or digital notepad.

Personas

The Average Cook

Frustrations She currently saves her grocery list on her phone's Notes app. Nothing is categorized and she constantly goes to the same aisle multiple times to get all of her items.

Goals She would love an app that organizes her grocery list by type, so that she knows what items would be in a certain aisle or part of the store.

The Party Host

Frustrations She likes to cook for her potluck parties, but her friends bring fast food. She needs to find a compromise with her friends in order to get rid of the constant fast food that keeps showing up to the party!

Goals To compromise, she is willing to cook all the food if her friends buy the groceries. She would like an app that has a sharing function to fix her fast-food issue!

Brand Design

The Brand Name

There were many names I was considering, but "Cauldron" stood out to be the most interesting. When you think of the word "Cauldron" these descriptions come to mind:
  • A melting pot full of different items
  • Magic, mysticality
  • People creating a concoction/recipe

Font Selection

Because there is a lot of information within the app, I wanted to keep the font choice simple. The font I chose is IBM Plex Sans. While it is nice and simple, there is still character to this sans-serif font.

High Fidelity Mockups

The first iteration consisted of including all the screens needed, but they needed to be designed better and designed of higher quality.
I decided to rearrange the layout for the second iteration, and it transformed the look and feel.
The third iteration was related to accessibility issues. For example, the search bar used to be a semi-transparent white with a medium grey text. Unfortunately, that was not accessible. I changed the background to a mandarin color with dark grey font, and that passes AAA accessibility.

Conclusion

Overall

Creating a minimum viable product required many iterations. Designing the right layout was important to the usability of the app.

What I would've done different

Keeping accessibility in mind from the beginning is very important and can save a lot of time during screen updates.

If I had more time

I would deploy this product and measure the amount of time a user saves with this app.
